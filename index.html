THE WEEKLY PLATE

Discover delicious, easy-to-follow recipes tailored for every day of the week. From breakfast to dinner, elevate your meals with fresh ideas and weekly inspiration.

monday

Kick off the week with a fruity yogurt breakfast, grilled chicken for lunch, and warming vegetable soup for dinner.

Code

  • Breakfast - Yogurt with Granola and Fruits

    • Yogurt
    • Granola
    • Strawberries
    • Banana
    • Apple
    • Honey

  • Lunch - Grilled Chicken Breast with Mashed Potatoes and Spinach Salad

    • Chicken breast
    • Olive oil
    • Salt and pepper
    • Potatoes
    • Milk (or cream)
    • Butter
    • Spinach
    • Tomato

  • Dinner - Vegetable Soup

    • Carrot
    • Potato
    • Zucchini
    • Vegetable broth
    • Onion
    • Garlic
    • Whole-grain bread

MEAL PREPARATION FOR DAY 1

For Day 1 breakfast, start by gathering yogurt, granola, strawberries, banana, apple, and honey. Wash the strawberries, slice them, and do the same for the banana and apple. In a bowl, add a serving of yogurt and top it with granola. Then, arrange the sliced fruits on top and drizzle some honey for added sweetness. Serve immediately and enjoy this refreshing and nutritious breakfast!

For lunch, prepare grilled chicken breast with mashed potatoes and a spinach salad. Season the chicken breast with olive oil, salt, and pepper, then grill it on medium-high heat for about 6-7 minutes on each side until fully cooked. Meanwhile, peel and dice potatoes, boil them in salted water until tender, then mash them with milk and butter until smooth. For the spinach salad, wash the spinach, tear it into bite-sized pieces, and combine it with sliced tomato, drizzled with olive oil and seasoned with salt and pepper. Plate the grilled chicken alongside the creamy mashed potatoes and fresh salad for a satisfying meal.

For dinner, create a hearty vegetable soup using carrots, potatoes, zucchini, vegetable broth, onion, and garlic. Begin by peeling and dicing the carrots and potatoes, then chopping the zucchini and mincing the onion and garlic. In a large pot, heat olive oil and sauté the onion and garlic until translucent. Add the diced carrots and potatoes, cooking for about 5 minutes before pouring in the vegetable broth. Bring it to a boil, add the zucchini, and simmer until all the vegetables are tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve the hot soup with slices of whole-grain bread on the side for a comforting end to the day.

Enjoy avocado toast in the morning, spaghetti Bolognese at lunch, and a light spinach omelette to end the day.

  • Dinner - Spinach and Cheese Omelette

    • Eggs

MEAL PREPARATION FOR DAY 2

For Day 2 breakfast, begin by gathering your ingredients, which include bread, a ripe avocado, an egg, salt, pepper, and olive oil (optional). Start by toasting a slice of bread until it’s golden brown. While the bread is toasting, cut the avocado in half, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl. Mash the avocado with a fork, seasoning it with salt and pepper. To poach the egg, bring a small pot of water to a gentle simmer, adding a splash of vinegar if desired. Crack the egg into a small bowl and gently slide it into the water, cooking for about 3-4 minutes until the white is set but the yolk remains runny. Once the bread is toasted, spread the mashed avocado on top, place the poached egg, and season with additional salt and pepper. Serve immediately with a cup of tea on the side.

For lunch, prepare spaghetti Bolognese by gathering your ingredients: spaghetti, ground beef, onion, garlic, tomato sauce, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Start by boiling water in a large pot and cooking the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat, adding diced onion and minced garlic until softened. Then, add the ground beef and cook until browned, draining any excess fat if necessary. Stir in the tomato sauce, season with salt and pepper, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes. Once the spaghetti is cooked and drained, toss it with the Bolognese sauce until well coated, and serve hot, optionally garnished with grated cheese and fresh basil.

For dinner, make a spinach and cheese omelette served with cucumber slices. Gather your ingredients: eggs, fresh spinach, cheese, cucumber, olive oil or butter, salt, and pepper. Start by washing and roughly chopping the spinach and slicing the cucumber. In a bowl, whisk the eggs with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil or butter in a non-stick skillet over medium heat, adding the spinach and sautéing until wilted. Pour the whisked eggs over the spinach and cook until the edges begin to set. Sprinkle cheese on top, fold the omelette in half, and continue cooking until the cheese melts and the eggs are fully cooked. Serve the omelette hot alongside the refreshing cucumber slices for a light and satisfying dinner.

MEAL PREPARATION FOR DAY 3

For Day 3 breakfast, start by gathering your ingredients: rolled oats, water or milk, an apple, cinnamon, and optional sugar. In a pot, combine the rolled oats and water or milk in a 1:2 ratio (1 cup oats to 2 cups liquid). Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally until it thickens. While the oatmeal is cooking, wash the apple and slice it into thin rounds or wedges, sprinkling them with cinnamon and a bit of sugar if desired. Once the oatmeal is ready, pour it into a bowl and top it with the cinnamon-sprinkled apple slices for a warm and hearty breakfast.

For lunch, prepare baked fish served with rice and steamed broccoli. Gather your ingredients, including fish fillets (such as salmon or tilapia), rice, broccoli, olive oil, lemon, salt, and pepper. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and place the fish fillets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Drizzle with olive oil, season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice, then bake for about 15-20 minutes or until the fish flakes easily with a fork. Rinse the rice under cold water, then combine it with water in a pot according to the package instructions. While the rice cooks, bring a small pot of water to a boil and steam the broccoli for about 5-7 minutes until tender. Plate the baked fish alongside the cooked rice and steamed broccoli for a nutritious and balanced lunch.

For dinner, make a turkey breast sandwich with lettuce, tomato, and cheese on whole-grain bread. Gather sliced turkey breast, lettuce, tomato, cheese, whole-grain bread, and optional mayonnaise or mustard. Start by washing and drying the lettuce leaves, then slice the tomato. Take two slices of whole-grain bread and spread mayonnaise or mustard on one side if desired. Layer with slices of turkey breast, followed by lettuce and tomato, then add cheese on top before closing the sandwich with the other slice of bread. Cut the sandwich in half and serve it with your favorite side, such as chips or a small salad, for a satisfying end to the day.

MEAL PREPARATION FOR DAY 4

For Day 4 breakfast, start by gathering your ingredients: a banana, a mango, fresh spinach, almond milk (or any milk of your choice), honey (optional), and ice cubes (optional). Peel and slice the banana and chop the mango into small pieces. In a blender, combine the banana, mango, fresh spinach, and almond milk. If desired, add honey for sweetness and ice cubes for a chilled smoothie. Blend until smooth, then pour the smoothie into a glass and enjoy your nutritious breakfast!

For lunch, prepare a Caesar salad with grilled chicken and croutons. Gather your ingredients: romaine lettuce, grilled chicken breast, Caesar dressing, croutons, optional Parmesan cheese, and black pepper. If the chicken is not already cooked, grill the chicken breast until fully cooked and slice it into strips. Wash and chop the romaine lettuce into bite-sized pieces. In a large bowl, combine the lettuce, grilled chicken, and croutons, then drizzle with Caesar dressing and toss to combine. Plate the salad and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese and black pepper, if desired, for a satisfying lunch.

For dinner, make a cream of pumpkin soup with sunflower seeds and bread. Gather your ingredients: pumpkin (fresh or canned), onion, garlic, vegetable broth, olive oil, sunflower seeds, whole-grain bread, salt, and pepper. If using fresh pumpkin, peel, seed, and chop it into cubes. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat and add chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until soft. Add the pumpkin cubes (or canned pumpkin) and vegetable broth, bringing the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until the pumpkin is tender (about 15-20 minutes). Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree the soup until smooth and season with salt and pepper to taste. Ladle the soup into bowls, garnish with sunflower seeds, and serve hot with slices of whole-grain bread for a comforting dinner.

MEAL PREPARATION FOR DAY 5

For Day 5 breakfast, begin by gathering your ingredients: whole wheat pancake mix, ripe bananas, honey, and optional toppings such as syrup or fresh fruit. Prepare the pancake batter according to the package instructions, adding mashed bananas to the mix for added flavor and nutrition. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and pour in the batter, cooking until bubbles form on the surface. Flip the pancakes and cook until golden brown. Once ready, serve the pancakes stacked high, topped with additional banana slices and a drizzle of honey for a delicious start to your day.

For lunch, prepare a dish of stewed lentils served with rice and avocado salad. Gather your ingredients: dried lentils, rice, onion, garlic, bell pepper, olive oil, spices (like cumin and paprika), and fresh avocado. Rinse the lentils under cold water and set aside. In a pot, heat olive oil over medium heat and sauté chopped onion, garlic, and bell pepper until softened. Add the rinsed lentils, water, and spices, bringing the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for about 25-30 minutes until the lentils are tender. While the lentils cook, prepare the rice according to the package instructions. For the avocado salad, dice the avocado and mix it with lime juice, salt, and diced tomatoes. Serve the stewed lentils alongside the rice and avocado salad for a hearty and nutritious lunch.

For dinner, make a tuna salad with lettuce, grated carrot, and sweet corn. Gather your ingredients: canned tuna, lettuce, grated carrot, sweet corn, mayonnaise, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. In a bowl, drain the canned tuna and flake it with a fork. In another bowl, combine the tuna, grated carrot, and sweet corn, then add mayonnaise and a squeeze of lemon juice to taste. Season with salt and pepper, mixing until well combined. Serve the tuna salad over a bed of fresh lettuce for a light and satisfying dinner.

MEAL PREPARATION FOR DAY 6

For Day 6 breakfast, start by gathering your ingredients: strawberries, kiwi, orange, yogurt, and granola. Begin by washing the strawberries and slicing them into halves or quarters. Peel and slice the kiwi and orange into bite-sized pieces. In a bowl, layer the yogurt, followed by a generous topping of fresh fruit. Sprinkle granola on top for added crunch and texture. Serve immediately for a refreshing and nutritious breakfast to kickstart your day.

For lunch, prepare oven-baked beef with steamed potatoes and coleslaw. Gather your ingredients: beef cuts (like sirloin or flank), potatoes, cabbage, carrots, mayonnaise, vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Season the beef with salt and pepper, then place it in a baking dish. Bake for about 25-30 minutes or until cooked to your desired doneness. While the beef is baking, peel and chop the potatoes, then steam them until tender. For the coleslaw, thinly slice the cabbage and grate the carrots. In a bowl, combine the cabbage and carrots, then dress with mayonnaise, vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat. Serve the oven-baked beef with steamed potatoes and coleslaw for a fulfilling lunch.

For dinner, create a chicken wrap filled with spinach, tomato, and a sprinkle of cheese. Gather your ingredients: cooked chicken breast, whole wheat wraps, fresh spinach, tomatoes, cheese (such as cheddar or feta), and any preferred sauces (like ranch or salsa). Start by slicing the cooked chicken breast into strips. Lay a whole wheat wrap flat and layer with fresh spinach, sliced tomatoes, and chicken strips. Sprinkle cheese over the top and drizzle with your choice of sauce. Roll the wrap tightly, securing the filling, and slice it in half to serve. Enjoy this delicious wrap for a light and satisfying dinner.

sunday

Savor scrambled eggs and mushrooms in the morning, grilled chicken with asparagus for lunch, and cozy tomato basil soup for dinner.

Code

  • Breakfast - Scrambled Eggs with Spinach and Mushrooms

    • Eggs
    • Spinach
    • Mushrooms
    • Whole-grain bread

  • Lunch - Grilled Chicken with Asparagus and Sweet Potato Purée

    • Chicken breast
    • Asparagus
    • Sweet potatoes
    • Olive oil
    • Salt and pepper

  • Dinner - Tomato Basil Soup

    • Canned tomatoes (or fresh tomatoes)
    • Basil
    • Vegetable broth
    • Onion
    • Garlic
    • Whole-grain roll

MEAL PREPARATION FOR DAY 7

For Day 7 breakfast, begin by gathering your ingredients: eggs, fresh spinach, mushrooms, and whole-grain bread. Start by washing and chopping the spinach and slicing the mushrooms. In a non-stick skillet, heat a little olive oil over medium heat, then add the mushrooms and sauté until they soften. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and pour them into the skillet, stirring gently until scrambled and fully cooked. Serve the scrambled eggs with sautéed spinach and mushrooms alongside toasted whole-grain bread for a hearty and nutritious breakfast.

For lunch, prepare grilled chicken served with asparagus and sweet potato mash. Gather your ingredients: chicken breast, fresh asparagus, sweet potatoes, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Season the chicken breast with olive oil, salt, and pepper, then grill for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through. While the chicken is grilling, peel and chop the sweet potatoes, then boil them until tender. Drain and mash the sweet potatoes, adding a touch of olive oil, salt, and pepper to taste. For the asparagus, toss it in olive oil, salt, and pepper, then grill for about 5 minutes until tender. Plate the grilled chicken alongside the asparagus and sweet potato mash for a balanced and satisfying lunch.

For dinner, make a comforting tomato soup garnished with fresh basil and served with whole-grain rolls. Gather your ingredients: canned tomatoes, vegetable broth, onion, garlic, basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper. In a pot, heat olive oil over medium heat, then add chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until fragrant. Pour in the canned tomatoes and vegetable broth, bringing the mixture to a simmer. Cook for about 20 minutes to blend the flavors. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, then season with salt and pepper. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil, and enjoy with warm whole-grain rolls for a cozy dinner.
